# Launch Strategy

## The Launch Mindset
Launch is the beginning of learning, not the end of building. Ship when the core loop works, not when every feature is done.

## ASO — App Store Optimization

**Title** (most important): Brand - Primary Keyword. 30 chars. Think "what do users search when they have the problem you solve?"

**Screenshots** (conversion rate): First screenshot must communicate core value in 3 seconds. Show outcomes ("Your parent's stories, preserved"), not features ("Recording feature").

**Description**: First 3 lines visible before "more" — treat as ad copy. Lead with the user's pain. Social proof early. Feature bullets at the bottom.

**Keywords (iOS)**: 100 chars, no spaces after commas, no words already in title, no competitor names.

## Launch Sequencing
- **T-30 days**: App submitted for review, landing page live with email capture
- **T-14 days**: TestFlight beta with 10-20 real users, incorporate feedback
- **T-7 days**: Marketing assets finalized, launch posts drafted
- **Day 0**: Post in 2-3 communities simultaneously, respond to every early comment personally
- **T+7 days**: Reply to every App Store review, send "we launched" email

## First 100 Users (ranked by effectiveness)
1. Personal network (highest conversion, limited scale)
2. Relevant subreddits (high intent, needs authentic participation)
3. ProductHunt (exposure, low conversion)
4. Twitter/X (if you have an audience)
5. App Store organic (slow build, compounds)

Acquire first 100 manually. Automation comes later.

## Post-Launch Iteration
- Week 1: Listen to everything
- Week 2-4: Fix top 3 complaints
- Month 2: Ship top requested feature
- Month 3: Focus on retention, not acquisition

Fix retention before scaling acquisition.
