# PITH — Brand Identity

## Name
PITH

Written in all caps as a wordmark.
Never: "Pith", "pith", "PithHQ", "Pith HQ".
Domain and handles use lowercase: pithhq.com, @pithhq, github.com/pithhq

## What PITH Means
The pith of something is its essential core — the most important 
part, distilled from everything surrounding it. "Pithy" means 
expressing much in few words: precise, dense with meaning, nothing 
wasted.

PITH does to your documents what a good editor does to a draft — 
extracts what matters and leaves the noise behind.

## Tagline
"Your knowledge, compiled."

Alternatives in reserve:
- "Knowledge that compounds."
- "The pith of everything you know."

## Positioning
PITH is not a search engine for your files.
PITH is not a chat interface on top of your documents.
PITH is a knowledge compiler — it reads your sources once,
builds something richer than the originals, and gets smarter
every time you add something new.

The distinction that matters: RAG retrieves. PITH compiles.

## Voice and Tone
**Precise.** Every word earns its place. No filler, no hedging,
no "leverage" or "empower". If it can be cut, cut it.

**Honest.** No overpromising. PITH does specific things well.
State what it does, state what it doesn't. Trust the reader.

**Direct.** Address the user as someone capable and intelligent.
No hand-holding language. No exclamation marks.

**Calm.** Not excited about AI. Not breathless about the future.
Steady, considered, built to last.

What PITH sounds like: a good technical book. Merriam-Webster.
A senior engineer explaining something clearly.

What PITH does not sound like: a SaaS landing page. A VC pitch.
A product that calls itself "revolutionary".

## Audience
Primary: Solopreneurs and small teams in knowledge-intensive fields.
Consultants, lawyers, researchers, agency owners, indie studios.

They are: intelligent, busy, skeptical of AI hype, protective of
their data, frustrated by tools that promise much and deliver noise.

They are not: early adopters chasing the latest thing. They want
something that works reliably and stays out of their way.

## Visual Identity

### Wordmark
Monospace font, 32px+, letter-spacing 0.2em, all caps.
Dark version: #E8E5D8 on #111110 background.
Light version: #1C1B18 on #F7F6F2 background.
Never use a logo or icon — wordmark only at this stage.

### Colour Palette
Primary background:  #111110  — Obsidian
Secondary surface:   #1C1B18  — Surface
Light background:    #F7F6F2  — Parchment
Accent:              #B07858  — Copper
Success / health:    #5C8A5A  — Sage
UI / tags:           #E0DDD0  — Stone

Accent usage: copper appears on warnings, entity tags,
CLI highlights, and active states. Never on success
states (sage only) or errors (system red only).

### Typography
Display:  sans-serif, 28px, weight 500
Body:     sans-serif, 15px, weight 400, line-height 1.7
Mono:     monospace, 13px — CLI output and metadata only

### Aesthetic Direction
Sparse, typographic-first, dark mode primary.
No gradients. No illustrations of brains or neural networks.
No glowing orbs or abstract data visualizations.
Reference: Stripe Docs, Linear, Obsidian.

### CLI Output
Terse. No emoji. Color only where it aids comprehension.
Copper for warnings. Sage for success. System red for errors.
Progress messages are factual, not cheerful.

Good: "3 pages updated · 1 created · 0 conflicts"
Bad: "🚀 Processing your amazing document!"

## Competitor Positioning
Against RAG tools (NotebookLM, VaultMind, ChatGPT file uploads):
"They retrieve. PITH compiles. Ask again in six months —
their knowledge base is identical. Yours has been growing."

Against general wikis (Notion, Confluence):
"You write those. PITH writes itself."

Against cloud AI tools:
"Your documents stay on your machine. Full stop."
